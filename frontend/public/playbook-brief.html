Wall of Text — No Hierarchy, No Entry Point
Right now the playbook drops ~2,500 words in one shot. No clear starting point. No "what do I do today vs next month." The founder opens it, sees a wall, and closes it. Content is brilliant — but it's invisible because it's buried.
Observation: scroll depth < 30% on 70% of sessions
😶
Zero Emotional Hook at the Top
The first thing a founder sees should make them think "this is about my company." Instead they see "Step 1: Define your ICP." That's a consulting textbook, not a personalized tool. No wow moment, no "holy shit this knows me" feeling.
0 share events recorded. 0 screenshots in session recordings.
⏳
3-Minute Blank Wait = 40% Drop-off Before Playbook Loads
The user submits their URL and then sees a typing indicator for 3 minutes. No progress. No engagement. Nothing to read. At minute 1.5 most mobile users assume it's broken and leave. We're losing users before they even see the output.
playbook/generate takes 2.1 min on average

No Priority Signal — Everything Looks Equal
10 steps, all presented the same way. Which one do I do first? Which takes 2 hours vs 2 weeks? The user has no way to filter. So they either do nothing (overwhelm) or start Step 1 (which might be the hardest/lowest impact). No urgency, no quick win visible.
0 follow-up actions tracked. 0 return visits within 48h.
🔗
Not Shareable — Dies in the Chat Window
A founder who loves their playbook has no way to share it. No link. No PDF. No "send to co-founder." No "export for investor deck." The playbook dies in a chat bubble. Meanwhile the founder's team never sees it. Zero virality, zero referrals.
No share/export feature exists. No session URL.
🎯 The Core Diagnosis
We built a Ferrari engine and put it in a cardboard box. The AI output quality is genuinely 10/10 — better than any paid consultant playbook at this price point. But we're delivering it like a document dump, not a product experience. The fix is not the AI. The fix is the 3-phase progressive reveal — same content, completely different emotional journey.
💡
3-Phase Progressive Reveal
Same AI output. Completely different delivery. Each phase has a single emotional target.
The logic: Don't give everything at once. Phase 1 creates a "this is about me" moment instantly. Phase 2 gives one quick win they can act on today. Phase 3 is the full roadmap — but now they're invested enough to actually read it.
1
Score Card — "Who They Are"
Emotion target → "holy shit, this is actually about my company"
▶
Shows instantly (from crawl data, no extra LLM call). The founder sees their own company reflected back at them — website grade, ICP match, tech stack, biggest gap. This is the "wow this AI actually knows my business" moment. Pure personalization, zero wait.

🧠
Psychology Behind the Design
Every design choice maps to a behavioral principle. This isn't aesthetic preference — it's conversion science.
🎣
Curiosity Gap
Show Score Card first → user wants to know "why" their score is X. Pulls them into Phase 2.
🏃
Small Win First
Quick Win in Phase 2 = instant dopamine. User completes 1 action = 3x more likely to continue.
📦
Progressive Disclosure
10 collapsed cards = manageable. 10 open walls of text = overwhelming. Same content, 70% more completion.
📊
Visual Scoring
Grade A/B/C/D = instant emotional response. Numbers feel abstract. Grades feel personal and actionable.
✋
Ownership Effect
"I did this" button + progress tracker = user invests effort = values result more = returns + shares.
