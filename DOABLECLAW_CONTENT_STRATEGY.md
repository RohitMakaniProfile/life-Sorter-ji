## Executive Summary

DoableClaw's playbook output is not just a product feature — it is a **content engine**. Every single time the tool runs on a website, it produces 7 distinct, ready-to-post content assets that can drive organic reach, brand recall, and user acquisition across LinkedIn, Twitter/X, Instagram, and WhatsApp.

This document maps each piece of playbook output to a specific content strategy, post format, platform, and expected audience reaction.

**Core Thesis:**
> The product IS the marketing. Every audit = 7 posts. Run 1 audit per day = 200+ posts per month. Zero ad spend required.

## Platform-Specific Notes

### LinkedIn
- Best for: Value Bomb, Army Post, Progress Post
- Format: Single image post or short text + screenshot
- Hook: Lead with a number ("₹5.8L/month revenue leak")
- Audience: Founders, marketers, growth teams

### Twitter / X
- Best for: Shock Post, Fear Post, Callout Post
- Format: Thread (7–10 tweets) or single tweet + screenshot
- Hook: Bold claim first, explanation after
- Audience: Startup community, indie hackers, product builders

### Instagram / Reels
- Best for: Before/After H1, Week 1 Blueprint (visual)
- Format: Carousel (swipe-through) or 60-second screen recording
- Hook: "Watch AI roast this website in 60 seconds"
- Audience: D2C founders, small business owners

### WhatsApp / Telegram
- Best for: Fear Post, Value Bomb (forward-bait)
- Format: Clean screenshot — no text needed, image speaks
- Hook: Score card image — people forward naturally
- Audience: Business owner groups, founder communities

## The "Roast a Brand" Campaign

**Concept:** Every week, publicly audit 1 well-known Indian startup or D2C brand. Post results. Tag them (or don't — controversy works either way).

**Week 1:** Mamaearth
**Week 2:** boAt
**Week 3:** Lenskart
**Week 4:** Razorpay

**Expected outcome:**
- Brands respond (good or bad = free reach)
- Other founders wonder "what would MY score be?"
- Media picks it up ("AI tool auditing Indian brands")
- DoableClaw = credibility + brand recall

**Risk management:** Frame it as educational ("we see an opportunity here"), not a takedown ("this brand sucks"). Stay data-driven — the score speaks for itself.
